Discover some of the most historic and scenic destinations in Saudi Arabia. From ancient wells to majestic mountains, each location showcases the Kingdom’s rich heritage and natural beauty.

Bir Hadaj – Tayma (Tabuk)

Bir Hadaj is one of the oldest and largest wells in the Arabian Peninsula, surrounded by palm trees. It dates back to the 6th century BCE and has been restored several times. Water still flows today to irrigate farms and camels.

Famous Dish: Al-Majalala – slow-cooked meat with spices and vegetables, served with bread or rice.

Ajā & Salma Mountains – Hail

Ajā and Salma are twin mountains near Hail, the highest peaks of the Najd plateau. Known for their green valleys, Talh and Sidr trees, and stunning landscapes. Their names come from a famous pre-Islamic love story.

Famous Dish: Kabsa with lamb, Jareesh with dates, Arabic coffee, and Haneeth.
