Maqla Tumayya (Al-Wahbah Crater) – Taif Region


Al-Wahbah Crater — top view showing the white salt floor

Al-Wahbah Crater at sunset with palm trees

About the Place

Maqla Tumayya, also known as Al-Wahbah Crater, is one of the most remarkable natural landmarks in Saudi Arabia and the Middle East. Located approximately 254 km northeast of Taif city, near Harrat Kishb in the Mecca Region, this massive volcanic crater stretches about 3 km in diameter and plunges roughly 380 meters deep.

At the bottom lies a white salt flat of sodium phosphate crystals. Scientists agree it is a maar crater formed about 1.15 million years ago. In 2024, UNESCO and the IUGS ranked it among the world's top 100 geological heritage sites.

The Legend of Tumayya

“He snatched my heart like the snatching of Tumayya.” — Prince Khalid Al-Faisal

According to Arabian legend, a female mountain named Tumayya fell in love with a male mountain named Qatan. A jealous mountain called Ukkash struck her with a spear. She fell, and her fall carved the great crater we see today.

Best Time to Visit

The best time is October to March. The hike down takes 45–60 minutes and back up takes 60–90 minutes. Bring plenty of water and sturdy hiking boots. Early morning offers the most spectacular views.

Famous Food in Taif

  • Al-Saleeg: White rice cooked in meat broth and milk with cardamom and cinnamon, topped with grilled chicken and ghee. The most iconic Taif dish.
  • Al-Hareesa: Wheat and meat slow-cooked into a smooth paste with cinnamon and cardamom. A beloved Ramadan dish.
  • Al-Thareed: Thin flatbread soaked in rich meat broth with vegetables and spices.
  • Taif Mountain Honey: Harvested from the wild mountain flowers of Al-Shafa, considered among the finest honeys in the world.
  • Rose Water Sweets & Coffee: Taif is the “City of Roses.” Rose water flavors coffee, desserts, and drinks.
  • Taif Fruits: The “Garden of the Arabian Peninsula” — famous grapes, pomegranates, figs, and peaches.

Notable Flowers and Plants

  • Taif Rose (Rosa × damascena): Blooms once a year in spring. 4 tons of petals produce just 1 kg of rose oil. UNESCO recognized it as intangible cultural heritage.

    Freshly harvested Taif Roses ready for distillation
  • Huernia (Carrion Flower): A strange succulent with star-shaped patterned flowers that mimic the smell of rotting meat to attract flies for pollination. Found in Taif's volcanic rocks.
  • Adenium obesum (Desert Rose): A bizarre succulent with a swollen trunk and bright pink trumpet-shaped flowers. Thrives in Taif's rocky terrain.
  • Sidr (Ziziphus spina-christi): Ancient thorny tree whose nectar produces prized Sidr honey.
  • Mountain Thyme (Za'atar): Wild thyme from the Taif highlands used in cooking and herbal teas.
Quick Facts — Maqla Tumayya

Diameter: approximately 3 km

Depth: approximately 380 meters

Distance from Taif: 254 km northeast

Rare Minerals: Olivine, Zeolite, Phosphate

UNESCO/IUGS: Top 100 Geological Heritage Sites (2024)

Administrative Region: Al-Muwaih Governorate, Mecca Region

Lake Al-Asfar (Yellow Lake) – Al-Ahsa, Eastern Province

About the Place

Lake Al-Asfar (“Yellow Lake”) is a seasonal natural lake in Al-Ahsa Governorate, Eastern Province, about 12 km east of Umran city. It sits within the Al-Ahsa Oasis, inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 2018 as the world's largest natural oasis.

It fills between January and April from the Al-Ahsa irrigation project serving over 22,000 farms. It attracts flamingos, herons, egrets, cormorants, and ducks. Declared a natural reserve by the Saudi government in 2019.

Best Time to Visit

Visit between January and April when the lake is full and flamingos are present. A 4x4 vehicle is recommended. The Saudi Tourism Authority lists it among the top 17 tourist destinations in the Kingdom.

Famous Food in Al-Ahsa (Eastern Province)

  • Al-Machboos Al-Ahsa: Fragrant basmati rice with saffron, dried limes, and spices, served with slow-cooked lamb or chicken.
  • Al-Harees: Wheat and meat cooked into a thick paste — a staple at Ramadan and Eid celebrations.
  • Al-Jereesh: Crushed wheat with meat, tomatoes, and spices — one of the oldest dishes in the Eastern Province.
  • Al-Mutabbaq: Crispy pastry filled with spiced minced meat, eggs, and onions — popular street food.
  • Al-Ahsa Dates (Khalas & Shalabi): World-renowned dates served at every meal alongside Arabic coffee.
  • Saffron Coffee (Qahwa): Arabic coffee flavored with saffron, cardamom, and rose water — the golden brew of the oasis.

Notable Plants and Wildlife

  • Date Palm (Phoenix dactylifera): Over 3 million date palms in Al-Ahsa Oasis — the world's largest concentration.
  • Yellow Mustard Flower (Brassica): Seasonal flower that turns surroundings golden yellow in winter — inspiring the lake's name.
  • Tamarisk and Reed Grasses: Provide shelter for flamingos and migratory birds around the lake.
  • Sidr Trees: Produce nectar for bees that yield prized Sidr honey.
Quick Facts — Lake Al-Asfar

Location: 12 km east of Umran, Al-Ahsa Governorate

Type: Seasonal natural lake (drainage & rainwater)

Best season: January – April

UNESCO Status: Part of Al-Ahsa Oasis World Heritage Site (2018)

Famous for: Flamingos, sand dunes, date palms, birdwatching

Natural Reserve since: 2019
